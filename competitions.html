Competitions

Explore each contest, quick facts, and official links.

ARML

Official Site
  • Team-based
  • Huntsville, AL
  • Late Spring

The American Regions Mathematics League is a national team competition held simultaneously across multiple sites. FLSAM (Florida) sends up to three teams each year and travels together—traditionally by bus—picking up students across the state for an unforgettable math road trip.

More details
  • Events include Team, Power, Individual, Relay, and Super Relay rounds.
  • Large, spirited arena setting with coordinated start times nationwide.
  • Great mix of collaboration, speed, and proof-style reasoning.

PUMaC

Official Site
  • Princeton, NJ
  • Fall
  • Algebra • Combo • Geo • NT

The Princeton University Mathematics Competition hosts top U.S. and international teams for a full day of problem solving across major topics. Expect tough problems, a fast pace, and a beautiful campus.

More details
  • Individual and team components, plus specialized subject rounds.
  • Problems emphasize creativity and depth over routine technique.
  • Great barometer for advanced contest readiness.

HMMT

Official Site
  • Cambridge, MA
  • February
  • Elite field (~1000 students)

The Harvard–MIT Mathematics Tournament is run by students at both schools and attracts one of the strongest fields in the world. FLSAM attends the February event hosted at MIT.

More details
  • Challenging individual + team rounds; fast relays; proof-style problems appear.
  • High energy, tightly-run logistics, and lots of math culture.
  • Excellent exposure to olympiad-adjacent problem styles.

CMIMC

Official Site
  • Pittsburgh, PA
  • Math + CS focus
  • Winter

The Carnegie Mellon Informatics and Mathematics Competition is student-run at CMU, blending rigorous mathematics with computational themes. Teams from across the U.S. gather for a full day of problem solving.

More details
  • Mixture of classic math topics with discrete/CS-flavored problems.
  • Rounds reward pattern spotting, proofs, and clean algorithmic thinking.
  • Great fit for students who enjoy both math and programming.

FLSAM is an organization of math competitors across the state of Florida who come together to focus on higher-level mathematics and train for contests. FLSAM has been active since 2003; it is currently incorporated and registered as a 501(c)(3).
